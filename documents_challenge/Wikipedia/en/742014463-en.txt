 Clarke County is a county of the U.S. state of Alabama. Its name is in honor of General John Clarke of Georgia. As of 2000, the population was 27,867. The county seat is Grove Hill. The county museum is housed in the Alston-Cobb House in Grove Hill.HistoryClarke County was established on 10 December 1812 by the Mississippi Territory. The county was declared a disaster area in September 1979 due to damage from Hurricane Frederic.Clarke County became a prohibition county, or dry county, in 1937. The people of Jackson voted to legalize alcohol sales on 10 May 2005, and Thomasville followed suit on 14 August 2007.GeographyAccording to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 3,244 km² (1,253 square miles).
