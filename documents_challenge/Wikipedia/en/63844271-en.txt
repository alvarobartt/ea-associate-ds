 Barbour County, Alabama is a county of the U.S. state of Alabama. Its name is in honor of James Barbour, who served as Governor of Virginia. As of 2000 the population was 29,038. Its county seat is Clayton.HistoryBarbour County was established on December 18, 1832 from former Creek Indian territory and a portion of Pike County.
