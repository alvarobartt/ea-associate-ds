thumb|right|250px|Saharan desert rose, 10 cm long. Desert rose is the colloquial name given to rosette formations of the minerals gypsum and barite with poikilotopic sand inclusions. The 'petals' are crystals flattened on the ''c'' crystallographic axis, fanning open along characteristic gypsum cleavage planes. (See also: Crystallography)The rosette crystal habit tends to occur when the crystals form in arid sandy conditions, such as the evaporation of a shallow salt basin.
