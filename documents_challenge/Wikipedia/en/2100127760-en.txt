Vladimir Oblast (, Vladimirskaya oblast) is a federal subject of Russia (an oblast). Its administrative center is the medieval Russian capital Vladimir, which is located 190&nbsp;km to the east from the present-day capital, Moscow. For detailed information about the area's medieval history, see Zalesye and Vladimir-Suzdal. The 12th-century cathedrals of Vladimir, Suzdal, Bogolyubovo, and Kideksha are inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.Area: 29,000&nbsp;km²; population: 1,523,990 (as of the 2002 Census).GeographyVladimir Oblast borders Moscow, Yaroslavl, Ivanovo, Ryazan, and Nizhny Novgorod Oblasts.Time zoneVladimir Oblast is located in the Moscow Time Zone (MSK/MSD).
