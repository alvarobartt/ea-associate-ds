In geometry and trigonometry, a right angle is an angle of 90 degrees, corresponding to a quarter turn (that is, a quarter of a full circle). It can be defined as the angle such that twice that angle amounts to a half turn, or 180° Lindahl, G. (1987). Euklides Geometri. Stockholm, Natur och kultur: ISBN 91-27-72185-X.Lines that are at a right angle to each other are perpendicular, an important geometrical property.
