Geigy AG | alma_mater = Universität Basel | doctoral_advisor = | doctoral_students = | known_for = Insecticidal applications of DDT | influences = | influenced = | prizes = Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (1948)  Paul Hermann Müller also known as Pauly Mueller (January 12, 1899 &ndash; October 12, 1965) was a Swiss chemist and Nobel laureate. In 1948 he received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his 1939 discovery of insecticidal qualities and use of DDT in the control of vector diseases such as malaria and yellow fever.Müller was born in Olten, Solothurn and was raised in Lenzburg, a village in Aargau. He received his PhD in 1925 and went to work for J. R. Geigy AG in Basel where he made his discovery in the autumn of 1939.
