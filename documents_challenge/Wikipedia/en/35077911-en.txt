 Cabo Delgado is the northernmost province of Mozambique. It has an area of 77,867 km² and a population of 1,650,270 (2006). Pemba is the capital of the province. As well as bordering the neighboring country of Tanzania, it borders the provinces of Nampula and Niassa. The region is an ethnic stronghold of the Makonde tribe. Macua and Mwani ethnic groups are also present.HistoryOn September 25, 1964, Frelimo guerrillas arrived from Tanzania, with help from some individuals of the surrounding population, attacked a Portuguese administrative post in the province.
